Pie Recipie

Ingredients

For the Filling:

  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 2 carrots, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 500g lamb mince
  • 2 tablespoons tomato purée
  • 1 tablespoon plain flour
  • 300ml beef stock
  • 1 tablespoon Worcestershire sauce
  • 1 teaspoon fresh thyme leaves (or 1/2 teaspoon dried thyme)
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper
  • 150g frozen peas

For the Mash:

  • 1kg potatoes, peeled and chopped
  • 50g butter
  • 100ml milk
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper

Instructions

1. Preparing the Filling:

  1. Heat the Olive Oil: In a large pan, heat the olive oil over medium heat.
  2. Sauté Vegetables: Add the chopped onion and carrots. Cook for 5-7 minutes until softened. Add the minced garlic and cook for another minute.
  3. Brown the Meat: Add the lamb mince to the pan and cook until browned all over.
  4. Add Tomato Purée and Flour: Stir in the tomato purée and flour, and cook for another 2 minutes.
  5. Add Stock and Seasoning: Pour in the beef stock, Worcestershire sauce, thyme, salt, and pepper. Bring to a simmer and cook for 20 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the mixture thickens.
  6. Add Peas: Stir in the frozen peas and cook for another 5 minutes.

2. Preparing the Mash:

  1. Cook the Potatoes: Place the potatoes in a large pot of salted water. Bring to a boil and cook until tender, about 15-20 minutes. Drain well.
  2. Mash the Potatoes: Return the drained potatoes to the pot. Add the butter and milk. Mash until smooth and creamy. Season with salt and pepper to taste.

3. Assembling the Shepherd's Pie:

  1. Preheat the Oven: Preheat your oven to 200°C (400°F).
  2. Layer the Filling: Spoon the lamb mixture into a baking dish, spreading it out evenly.
  3. Top with Mash: Spoon the mashed potatoes over the filling. Use a fork to spread it out and create a rough texture on top.
  4. Bake: Place the dish in the preheated oven and bake for 20-25 minutes, or until the top is golden brown and the filling is bubbling.

4. Finishing Touches:

  1. Rest and Serve: Let the Shepherd's Pie rest for a few minutes before serving. Enjoy hot.
